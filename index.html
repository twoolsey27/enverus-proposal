Nearly a year ago, we conducted the commercial diligence on Enverus. We know this business. But the world has shifted meaningfully since we were in the room.

Trillions destroyed, trillions created
$2T in traditional SaaS market cap wiped since August 2025. But in the same period, AI and data companies have seen massive value creation: Databricks hit $134B (up from $62B in 12 months), Anthropic reached $183B, and NVIDIA added $1T+ in market cap. The market is splitting. Companies that own proprietary data and embed AI into workflows are pulling ahead. Companies that just sell seats are getting punished. Enverus has the data. The question is whether it positions on the right side of this divide.
$2T destroyed in traditional SaaS. $1T+ created in AI/data.
Global supply is rewiring around geopolitical risk
Middle East conflict is increasing the call on US oil and gas resources - a direct tailwind for Enverus's core E&P data products (EAC, ENA). But domestically, Democrats may take the House and roll back O&G policy. OBBB already reversed renewable incentives that drove PET growth. The macro that supports Enverus today could look very different in 18 months.
Near-term US supply tailwind. Long-term policy uncertainty.
The barriers to entry have dropped, and new competitors are showing up
Six months ago, replicating Enverus's data products would take years and tens of millions. Today, AI tools let small teams build competitive offerings in months at a fraction of the cost. New startups are already targeting Enverus's space. The same capability shift is also Enverus's biggest opportunity: faster product development, AI-enabled workflows, new offerings on top of proprietary data. But the window to move first is closing.
New entrants building at a fraction of historical cost

Three Strategic Imperatives

Each imperative carries critical questions, and the world will keep generating new ones.

As energy and AI continue to evolve, Bain brings deep primary research, a proprietary expert network, and AI tools to help answer them fast. Some of the highest-value work is getting the right answer to a strategic question quickly, especially when it shapes an M&A decision or a portfolio bet. Our role: help answer these questions, start acting on the ones we can already see, and translate those answers into sized, prioritized initiatives.

How does Enverus protect its existing revenue base?

Enverus has real proprietary assets: EAC's curated L48 well-level data, ENA's two-sided network effects, deep workflow integration across thousands of customers. But AI disruption and new competitors are putting pressure on every line of the business. The product portfolio needs to get more defensible: built around what's truly proprietary, workflow-embedded, hard to rip out.
